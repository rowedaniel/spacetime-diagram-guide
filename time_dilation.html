Time Dilation

Previously, we briefly discussed how the ct and ct’ axes are not the same (unless v=0). To elaborate further on that idea, let’s select events A and B such that event A occurs for K at ct=0, x=0, and event B occurs at ct=n, x=0. One important aspect of this formula is that as v approaches 0, the denominator approaches 1, and the time experienced by K' becomes closer and closer to the time experience by K. This makes sense, because through our normal lives (not moving at relativistic speeds), we typically don't notice any time dilation.
