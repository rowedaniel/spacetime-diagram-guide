Lorentz Transformations

So we've talked about spacetime diagrams, but what about looking at things from different perspectives (inertial reference frames)?

Consider the following graph, using co-ordinate system K:

Notice that the point (red circle) is at x=0 regardless of time.

Let's say that the above graph represents a location on a train. From the perspective of everyone on the train, it doesn't look like the point is moving. Of course, from the point's perspective, it really doesn't look like it's moving.

Next, let's examine the same events A and B, but from the perspective of the emankment (co-ordinate system K').

ct'
x'
A
C

From the embankment's perspective, the train is moving, so it's natural that the two events shouldn't be in the same place/time. In this example, it looks like event A is observed at x'=0,ct'=0, and event C is observed at x'=0.5, ct'=1. Doing a simple division tells us that according to K', K seems to be moving Δx'/Δct' = (0.5-0) / (1-0) = 0.5, meaning Δx'/Δt' = (0.5)c, so K is moving half at the speed of light in the positive direction (relative to K'). You could thus also say that K' is moving at -c/2 relative to K. Notice that the second event in this diagram is not event B, but rather event C.
